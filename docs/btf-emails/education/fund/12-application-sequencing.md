**Subject (Fallback):** The 4-step funding sequence (and why most people botch it)

```
Hey {{first_name}},

Last email I gave you the lender shortlist. Now we sequence. This is where most people in the {{current_phase}} phase trip and never recover.

The wrong way: see a lender, apply. See another, apply. See another, apply. Three hard inquiries in two weeks. The third lender sees the first two inquiries and gets nervous -- "what does this person need money for so badly?" -- and tightens the offer or declines.

The right way: sequence applications in waves with rest periods between them.

WAVE 1: SAME-DAY STACK
Pick 2-3 lenders you are confident will approve. Apply on the same day, ideally within a 4-hour window. Why: most lenders pull credit but do not see inquiries that posted within the last 24-72 hours. You can stack 2-3 approvals before any of them see each other. This is legal and standard practice -- not a hack, just timing.

WAVE 2: 30-DAY REST
Stop. No applications. No inquiries. Let the new tradelines start reporting and let the inquiry cluster age. Your file looks "settled" by day 30.

WAVE 3: TIER UP
After 30 days with the new tradelines reporting, you can now apply to a tier-up lender (bigger limit, better terms). Their underwriter sees a recent positive history with the wave-1 lenders. Approval odds jump 30-50%.

WAVE 4: 60-90 DAY REST BEFORE NEXT MAJOR APPLICATION
For LOCs, SBA, or large equipment financing, let the file age 60-90 days after Wave 3. Then go for the big one.

The total timeline: 4-5 months from first application to closing on a $100K+ line of credit. That feels slow. It is not. Compare it to applying to everything in the same week, getting 6 declines, and waiting 24 months for the inquiries to fall off your report.

DO NOT:
- Apply same-day across more than 3 lenders. That is when it gets messy.
- Mix personal and business applications in the same wave. Separate the inquiries.
- Apply to MCA/ACH lenders during this sequence. They sell your data to other lenders within 24 hours and you will be buried in offers and inquiries.
```
