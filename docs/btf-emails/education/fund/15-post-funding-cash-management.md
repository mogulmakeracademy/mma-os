# Fund 15: The First 30 Days After Funding -- Setting Up Round Two

**Topic:** post_funding_cash_management
**Phase:** FUND | **Position:** 15 of 15 (final fund track email) | **Send Day:** 73

**Subject (Primary):** What you do in the first 30 days after funding decides round 2
**Subject (Fallback):** You got funded. Now do not blow it.

```
Hey {{first_name}},

You closed your first real round in the {{current_phase}} phase. Congrats. Now here is the part nobody talks about: what you do in the next 30 days determines whether round 2 happens in 6 months or 24 months.

Lenders watch what you do with the money. Every single one of them. They pull bank statements at renewal time and at the next application. If your file shows responsible deployment, the next round is bigger, faster, cheaper. If your file shows chaos, the door closes.

THE 30-DAY PLAYBOOK

DAY 1-7: SEGREGATE THE FUNDS
Move the funded amount into a separate operating account, not your main checking. Why: it stops you from emotional spending and gives you a clean paper trail showing exactly where every dollar went. Lenders love a clean trail.

DAY 1-30: DEPLOY ON THE STATED USE
Whatever you told the lender the money was for, spend it on that. If you said inventory, buy inventory. If you said equipment, buy equipment. If you said working capital, document the working capital cycle (payroll, AP, inventory) it covered. Lenders cross-check stated use against bank activity at renewal. Mismatched use is the #1 reason renewal applications get declined even when the business is doing well.

DAY 1-30: KEEP YOUR PRIMARY OPERATING ACCOUNT HEALTHY
Average daily balance in your main account should not drop dramatically the month after funding. If you funded $100K and your main account average balance drops from $40K to $5K, the underwriter wonders if you needed the money to cover a hole rather than to grow. Keep the main account average balance steady or growing.

DAY 15-30: PAY DOWN HIGH-COST DEBT FIRST IF APPLICABLE
If you have an MCA, payoff is priority one. If you have credit card balances above 30% utilization, pay them down. Every dollar of expensive debt you eliminate raises your score and improves your DSCR (debt service coverage ratio) for the next application.

DAY 30: DOCUMENT THE WIN
Pull a one-page summary: capital received, where it went, what it produced (revenue, inventory turn, new contracts, equipment installed). Save it. When you apply for round 2, you walk in with proof of execution. Most applicants walk in with a story. You walk in with a receipt.

THE RHYTHM FROM HERE
Round 1 funded. 90 days of clean deployment. Then start the round 2 sequence. Round 2 is typically 2-3x the size of round 1 when round 1 is deployed well. The compound starts here.

```
