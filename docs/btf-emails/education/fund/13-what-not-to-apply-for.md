Here is what they actually are and why they destroy businesses.

THE MATH
An MCA is not a loan. Legally, it is a sale of future receivables. They give you $50,000 today, you "sell" them $70,000 of your future credit card sales. They pull a percentage daily (often 10-15% of every deposit) until the $70,000 is paid back. That $20,000 difference, repaid in 6-9 months, is an APR of 70-180%. Not a typo. Some MCAs hit 350% APR when you do the real math.

ACH advances work the same way but pull fixed amounts daily from your operating account whether you had revenue that day or not. Miss a deposit, get an NSF, owe a fee, get hit with default interest. It compounds fast.

THE STACK TRAP
Here is the part nobody warns you about. The day you take an MCA, your contact info gets sold to 20-50 other MCA brokers. They will call you, email you, text you, daily, offering "additional capital." If you accept a second MCA on top of the first ("stacking"), your daily payment doubles. Most businesses hit 3-stack within 90 days and run out of cash. Default. Bank account frozen. Lawsuit. Personal guarantee enforced. Business dies.

I have watched this happen to dozens of people personally. The pattern is identical every time.

THE ONE EXCEPTION
There is exactly one scenario where an MCA makes sense: you have a guaranteed receivable closing within 30-60 days that exceeds the MCA payback, AND you have already exhausted every other option. That is it. Even then, take the smallest MCA possible and pay it off the second the receivable hits.
