Hey {{first_name}},

Most people think lenders read business loan applications top to bottom. They dont.

**An underwriter spends an average of 30 seconds on the first pass before deciding yes/maybe/no.** And in those 30 seconds, they pull THREE documents and look for THREE specific things.

**Document 1: Articles of Organization (or Incorporation)**
They check: Is this entity REAL, ACTIVE, and in a FUNDABLE STATE?

**Document 2: EIN Confirmation Letter (CP575 or 147C)**
They check: Does the EIN tie to the EXACT entity name on your articles? About 40% of files get flagged here.

**Document 3: Business Bank Statement (last 3 months)**
They check: Real cash flow? Real deposits? Is this a NEW account with low balance + few transactions (= shell entity)?

**That is it. 3 docs. 30 seconds. Then they decide whether to read further.**

**What is happening in your file this week:** if you have not uploaded these three, your coach will be asking. Get ahead of it.
