You are at the end of the stack track in the {{current_phase}} phase. Before we move into the FUND track next week, you need to know the actual numbers lenders look for. Not the marketing numbers. The underwriting numbers.

PAYDEX (D&B): aim for 80+
80 means "paying on terms." Below 80, you look like you pay late. Above 80, you look responsible. Above 90, you look excellent. The jump from 79 to 80 is the most important single-point move you will ever make on a business score.

INTELLISCORE PLUS (Experian Business): aim for 76+
Runs 1-100. Most cash lenders want 76 or higher. Below 50 is high-risk. 51-75 is medium-risk and you will get approved but at worse terms. 76+ unlocks the prime rates. Above 90 is rarefied air.

EQUIFAX BUSINESS DELINQUENCY SCORE: aim for 500+
Runs 224-580. Higher is better. 500+ is the threshold for most equipment financing. Below that and you are looking at second-tier lenders.

FICO SBSS (Small Business Scoring Service): aim for 160+ for SBA, 140+ for most lines of credit
This one is the gatekeeper for SBA loans. SBA minimum is 155, but most lenders want 160-170 before they will actually fund. Runs 0-300. Combines personal credit, business credit, and financial data into one score. You cannot pull this one as a consumer -- only lenders see it. But you can estimate it: if your personal FICO is 700+ and you have 3+ business tradelines reporting, you are probably at 155+.

PERSONAL FICO (still matters in STACK phase): aim for 720+
Until your business has 2 years of revenue and 5+ reporting tradelines, your personal score is part of the underwriting decision. 720+ unlocks the same approvals 760+ does for most business products.

Hit these numbers and the FUND phase opens up. Miss them and you will fight for every approval.
