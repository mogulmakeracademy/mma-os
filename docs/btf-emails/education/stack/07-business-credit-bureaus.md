Hey {{first_name}},

Quick gut check: do you know which business credit bureau your target lender actually pulls?

If the answer is no, you are flying blind in the {{current_phase}} phase. Let me fix that in 3 minutes.

There are three business bureaus that matter:

DUN & BRADSTREET (D&B)
This is the one most people obsess over because of PAYDEX. PAYDEX measures payment timing on a 0-100 scale. 80 = paying on terms. 100 = paying 30 days early. Net-30 vendors report here the heaviest. D&B is required for any kind of supplier credit, government contracts, or wholesale relationships. Get your DUNS number free -- never pay for it.

EXPERIAN BUSINESS
This is where most CASH lenders actually pull. Intelliscore Plus runs 1-100, and lenders want to see 76+. Store cards (Home Depot, Amazon Business), fleet cards, and small-business credit cards report here heaviest. If you are going after working capital or a line of credit, this is the bureau you optimize for.

EQUIFAX BUSINESS
The quietest of the three but climbing fast in 2025-2026. Used heavily by SBA-adjacent lenders, equipment financing companies, and some merchant service underwriters. Reports are spottier, but when they pull, they pull.

Here is the move: do not chase all three equally. Find out which bureau your next funding source pulls, then optimize for that one specifically. If you are going for a Chase Ink card, Experian matters most. If you are setting up wholesale with a supplier, D&B matters most. If you are looking at equipment financing through Crest or Balboa, Equifax matters most.
