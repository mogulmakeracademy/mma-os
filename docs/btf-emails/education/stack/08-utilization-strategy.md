# Stack 08: Utilization Timing -- The Statement Date Trick

**Subject (Fallback):** Why your utilization is wrong even when you pay in full

```
Hey {{first_name}},

If you pay your business cards in full every month and your scores still look mediocre, this email is for you.

Here is what nobody explains in the {{current_phase}} phase: utilization is reported on your STATEMENT DATE, not your due date. So even if you pay the full balance by the due date, the bureaus already saw whatever balance was sitting on the card when the statement closed.

Run a $9,000 charge through a $10,000 limit card. Pay it in full on the due date. Does not matter. The bureau already recorded 90% utilization on statement date. Your score takes the hit anyway.

The fix is simple but almost nobody does it:

1. Find your statement closing date for each card. It is on the statement, usually 21-25 days before the due date.
2. Pay the card DOWN to under 10% of the limit 3-5 days BEFORE statement date.
3. Let the statement post with low utilization showing.
4. Then pay any remainder by the due date as normal.

Result: bureaus see single-digit utilization. Your score reflects responsible use instead of looking maxed.

The target numbers to remember:
- Under 30% utilization: acceptable
- Under 10% utilization: optimal
- 1-3% utilization: shows active use without risk (best for FICO and Intelliscore)
- 0% utilization: actually slightly worse than 1-3% because it looks dormant

This works across personal and business credit. If you have a 750 personal score and a thin business profile, this trick alone can move your business cash-line approvals from "declined" to "approved" in 60 days -- no new accounts required.
```
